Questions people ask before signing up.

Is this actually free?
Yes — free during early access, and free forever for the core ledger, receipts, and PDF export. If FairSplit ever charges, it'll be for advanced features like multi-year archives or attorney-shareable links — never the core workflow. Early-access users are grandfathered in regardless.
What if my co-parent refuses to use it?
You can still use FairSplit solo. Most users start that way — log your shared expenses, attach receipts, and have a clean record ready. You can always invite them later, or just bring the PDF to mediation on your own.
Will the PDF actually hold up in court?
FairSplit is built for mediation and family-law attorney workflows — every entry is timestamped at creation, receipts can't be backdated, and the PDF includes a clean date range and category breakdown. The format is designed alongside family-law mediators. It's not a replacement for legal advice, but it gives you a far better record than Venmo notes.
How is this different from Splitwise?
Splitwise is for roommates and trips — it doesn't let you attach receipts, doesn't track expenses by child, and its exports aren't formatted for any professional context. FairSplit is built for one job: documenting shared kid expenses in a way that holds up if something gets contested.
When does early access open?
Rolling early access through summer 2026. Waitlist signups get the first invites and a free plan locked in for life. You'll get one personal email from me — Justin — when your invite is ready, plus an optional 10-minute call to ask what you'd want first.
Who can see my data?
Only you and the co-parent you invite. FairSplit doesn't sell data, doesn't run ads against your expense history, and doesn't share anything with attorneys, courts, or third parties unless you export the PDF and send it yourself. You can delete your account and all records at any time.
